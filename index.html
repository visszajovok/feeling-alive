My Why

Hello Everyone, I'm Mira, and I'm doing a half Ironman on May 17, exacly a day after my 18th birthday.

I was around 10 when one of my relatives did an Ironman. My mum told me about it, and I decided that day that when I was older, I'd become an Ironman too.

Six years later, I was sitting in a hospital, being diagnosed with chronic pain and told I should train less and take it easy. I didn't want to belive what they said.

Since pain joined my life, it slowly started to take away every part of me that I loved. In that moment, in the doctor’s office, I thought, "That’s it. This is my life now."

A few months earlier, when I first shared my idea with my family, they started asking, “Why? Why would you want to do it?”—so I thought a lot about it.

I came to realize that the feeling of slowly losing yourself and your control is almost unbearable. But it doesn’t have to be that way.

I remembered the little me, whose eyes were full of sparks when she first heard about this race.

I’m doing this for myself, and for everyone out there who feels limited and hopeless—because the only thing that’s stopping us IS US.

I hope I can encourage peopleto go after what they truly desire.

Do it scared. Do it inexperienced. Do it with doubt. Do it with pain. Just DO IT.

My pain is no longer stopping me — I will become an Ironman.
