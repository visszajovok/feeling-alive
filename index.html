My Why

Hello Everyone, I'm Mira, and I'm doing a half Ironman on May 17, exactly a day after my 18th birthday.

I was around 10 when one of my relatives did an Ironman. My mum told me about it, and I decided that day that when I was older, I’d become an Ironman too.

Six years later, I was sitting in a hospital, being diagnosed with chronic pain and told I should train less and take it easy. I didn’t want to believe what they said.

Since pain joined my life, it slowly started to take away everything I loved. In that moment, I thought: “That’s it. This is my life now.”

A few months earlier, when I first shared my idea with my family, they asked: “Why?” — so I thought a lot about it.

I realized that losing control of your own life is unbearable. But it doesn’t have to stay that way.

I remembered the little me — full of excitement — and decided to start from zero. Train for it. Take back control.

I’m doing this for myself, and for everyone who feels limited and hopeless — because the only thing stopping us is us.

I hope I can encourage people to go after what they truly want.

Do it scared. Do it inexperienced. Do it with doubt. Do it with pain. Just do it.

My pain is no longer stopping me — I will become an Ironman.
